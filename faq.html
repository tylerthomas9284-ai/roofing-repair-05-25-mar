Roof Repair & Replacement

During our inspection, we'll assess your roof's overall condition. Generally, if damage is localized (affecting less than 30% of the roof) and your roof is in otherwise good condition, repair is the best option. Full replacement is recommended if your roof is near the end of its lifespan (20-25 years for shingles), has widespread damage, or if repair costs approach 50% of replacement cost.

Most residential roof replacements are completed in 1-3 days, depending on the size and complexity of the roof, weather conditions, and unforeseen issues. We'll provide a specific timeline during your estimate.

All our work comes with a workmanship warranty (typically 5-10 years depending on the project). Additionally, the roofing materials we install are backed by manufacturer warranties ranging from 20-50 years or even lifetime warranties on premium products. We'll explain all warranty coverage during your estimate.

We make every effort to match your existing shingles as closely as possible. However, exact matches can be challenging due to weathering, aging, and discontinued products. We'll show you samples and discuss the best options during our inspection.

You don't need to be home during installation, though many homeowners prefer to be available at the start and end of each day. We'll discuss your preferences during scheduling. We do recommend securing pets indoors and removing vehicles from the driveway.

Insurance & Payment

Yes, we have extensive experience working with insurance companies on storm damage claims. We can help document damage, meet with adjusters, provide detailed estimates, and ensure you receive fair coverage for necessary repairs or replacement.

We accept cash, checks, and all major credit cards. For larger projects, we also offer financing options to make your roof replacement more affordable. Ask about our financing plans during your free estimate.

For roof replacements and larger projects, we typically require a deposit to order materials and schedule the job. The deposit amount and payment schedule will be clearly outlined in your contract. Final payment is due upon project completion and your satisfaction.

First, call your insurance company to report the damage. Then schedule an inspection with us. We'll document all damage and provide an estimate.
